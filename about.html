About CoderCM

The story behind making coding fun for kids

Meet the Creator

Hi, I'm Alexander Sichitiu, a third-year student-athlete at UNC Chapel Hill majoring in Computer Science. As someone who's always been passionate about technology and education, I wanted to combine my interests to create something meaningful that could help the next generation of young learners.

My journey into programming started with drag-and-drop interfaces, which made coding feel accessible and exciting rather than intimidating. This experience inspired me to develop CoderCM - a platform that brings that same joy and simplicity to teaching programming fundamentals to children.

The Vision

CoderCM was born from the belief that programming should be fun and approachable for everyone, especially children. Traditional coding can seem complex and overwhelming, but with tools like Alice, we can change that narrative. By using visual programming environments, kids can focus on creativity and problem-solving rather than getting stuck on syntax errors.

My goal is to democratize programming education, making it available to kids from all backgrounds and helping them discover the magic of creating with code. Whether they become professional developers or simply gain valuable logical thinking skills, the journey should be enjoyable and empowering.

Why Alice?

Alice stands out as the perfect tool for introducing programming to children because it eliminates the traditional barriers to entry. Instead of writing lines of text-based code, students work in a 3D environment where they can drag and drop objects, characters, and actions to create interactive stories and animations.

This approach builds confidence by allowing kids to see immediate results from their efforts. They learn fundamental programming concepts like variables, loops, and conditionals through hands-on experimentation, making abstract ideas concrete and understandable.
